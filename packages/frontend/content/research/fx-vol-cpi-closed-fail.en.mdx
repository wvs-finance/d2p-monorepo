The FX-vol-on-CPI-surprise iteration tested the hypothesis that Colombian CPI surprises predict weekly realised COP/USD volatility. The primary specification was Column-6 OLS of weekly RV^(1/3) on the AR(1)-filtered CPI surprise plus six controls, estimated on the Colombian weekly panel from 2008-01-07 to 2026-02-23 (n = 947 observations).

Headline result: β̂_CPI = -0.000685, HAC(4) SE = 0.001794, 90% CI = [-0.003635, 0.002265]. The primary T3b gate rule (β̂_CPI − 1.28·SE > 0 one-sided) is not satisfied — verdict FAIL. Auxiliary tests T1 (Mincer-Zarnowitz F consensus rationality) and T2 (Levene announcement channel) also fail. Seven sensitivity specifications complete the forest plot; two rows (A1 monthly cadence, A4 release-day-excluded) show 90% CI excluding zero in the positive direction, but the anti-fishing protocol prohibits their promotion for gate revision.

This failure disposition is retained in the catalog at equal narrative weight with passing iterations, in compliance with the lab's epistemic-honesty invariant: rejection is a result, not a researcher's failure.
